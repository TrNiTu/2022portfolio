My Experiences

Undergraduate Research Intern - UNL

Software Engineering Research and Empirical Studies Lab

January 2022 - Current



In this position, I was tasked with redesigning two websites: first of which was for the lab itself, SERESL, and the other was a project the lab was working on, iTrace. One of the most important skills I learned during my time in this role is how to manuever under strict time pressure; the release dates were strict the website was to be completed before the deadline so that the users may access the website for documentation, downloads, or other material that they may need. The websites were created using HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript with BootStrap as a CSS framework to allow for a flush and responsive web design. The use of Git, weekly meetings, and responsive communication involvement were extremely vital for success in this role.

Associate Developer - Design Studio

Jeffrey S. Raikes School of Computer Science and Management

August 2020 - May 2021



From full-stack mobile development to communicating with professionals in the real-world tech industry, this role allowed me to not only sharpen and challenge my software development skills, but also put me to the test under pressure against strict deadlines. In this role, I was able to utilize modern industry practices and paradigms to push and publish clean code that is able to be easily maintained by the sponsor I developed for. Some of the tools and languages we used were Typescript based React Native, Git, Xcode, Android Studio, and Figma. Working alongside a professional UI/UX engineer, my team and I were able to publish a fully functional mobile application for both Android and iOS devices alike. What assisted me in succeeding in this role was communicating the moment I or my teammates encountered roadblocks so that we may resolve the issue.
